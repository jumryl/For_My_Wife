Samgyupsal's
Date

We celebrated our monthsary at a samgyupsal restaurant after struggling to decide where to eat. We finally chose samgyupsal to satisfy her cravings, and coincidentally ran into some of our co-workers there. We sat at a separate table to enjoy our time together, and by the end of the meal, we were both extremely full. One of us even got a stomach ache from eating too much, so we took a taxi home. We ended the night laughing at each other as we slowly walked the rest of the way home, full and happy.

It was our monthsary, and as simple as it sounds, the hardest part of the day was deciding where to eat. We wandered around for a bit, going back and forth with suggestions, laughing at how indecisive we both were. But eventually, she smiled and said she was craving samgyupsal, and just like that, the decision was made. I couldn’t say no to that smile, and honestly, I was craving it too.

We made our way to one of our favorite samgyupsal spots, a familiar place that always felt warm and welcoming. As we walked in, we unexpectedly saw some of our co-workers dining there. They waved and greeted us cheerfully, and for a moment, it felt like a mini reunion. But since it was our special day, we kindly chose to sit at a separate table, wanting to have our own little moment together.

As soon as we sat down, the table quickly filled with plates of meat, fresh lettuce, and endless side dishes. We took turns grilling, feeding each other bites, and savoring the laughter between us. We talked about the little things, our day, memories from the past month, and dreams we hadn’t shared before. It was simple, but it felt so full of love. There was a kind of magic in that ordinary moment that made it unforgettable.

Without realizing it, we ended up eating more than we planned. We were so caught up in enjoying each other’s company that time, and portions, just slipped by. By the end, we leaned back, giggling over how full we were, holding our bellies and smiling at the memory we’d just made. One of us wasn’t feeling too well from eating a little too much, so we decided to take a taxi home to make things easier.

The ride was quiet and cozy, filled with soft glances and sleepy smiles. But the sweetest part came after, we slowly walked the rest of the way home, hand in hand, laughing at ourselves in the most loving way. There was no blame, no regret, just warmth. Just two people who enjoyed a little too much food, and a lot of love. It wasn’t a grand celebration, but it was ours. And somehow, it felt perfect.
